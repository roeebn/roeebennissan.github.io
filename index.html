Research

Sea-level rise and tetrapod biodiversity

I study how future sea-level rise will reshape ranges and risks for land tetrapods worldwide. I combine large-scale geospatial analysis, species distribution modelling, and climate projections to quantify exposure and guide conservation priority setting.

Synthetic biology and sustainable food systems

Earlier work focused on engineering E. coli for synthetic CO2 fixation and evaluating microbial protein as a climate-friendly alternative to beef and dairy through collaborations with IIASA and partners. This links molecular innovation to planetary-scale solutions.

About me

I am an environmental biology researcher working across scales. My current postdoctoral work in the labs of Uri Roll and Ron Milo investigates how sea-level rise will affect global tetrapod biodiversity. I use geospatial data, coastal DEMs, and climate projections to quantify risk and inform action.

During my MSc and PhD at the Weizmann Institute of Science I engineered E. coli for synthetic CO2 fixation. I later collaborated with the International Institute for Applied Systems Analysis (IIASA) to assess how microbial protein adoption could reduce land-use emissions and support biodiversity.
