Research

Sea-level rise and tetrapod biodiversity

I study how future sea-level rise will reshape ranges and risks for land tetrapods worldwide. I combine large-scale geospatial analysis, species distribution modelling, and climate projections to quantify exposure and guide conservation priority setting.

Sustainable systems

Linking molecular innovation to planetary-scale outcomes through collaborations on food systems, climate mitigation, and biodiversity conservation.

Focus areas

Food Systems & Microbial Protein

In collaboration with the International Institute for Applied Systems Analysis (IIASA), we assess the climate mitigation potential of microbial protein as a sustainable alternative to beef and dairy production. Microbial protein can be produced without fertile soil, uses fewer natural resources, and reduces pressure on land, biodiversity, and water systems. Its adaptability, high nutritional value, and potential for local production make it a promising solution for improving global food security while lowering environmental impacts.

Synthetic Biology

I apply synthetic biology to engineer microbes for sustainable applications, focusing on the production of value-added goods and on processes that reduce environmental impact. My work includes designing and optimizing metabolic pathways, enhancing microbial efficiency, and creating systems capable of utilizing alternative carbon sources. Through molecular biology, metabolic engineering, and computational modelling, I aim to develop microbial solutions that support a more sustainable and resilient future.

Climate–Biodiversity

Sea-level rise exposure of tetrapods using geospatial analysis, species distribution models, and coastal DEM harmonization to guide conservation priorities.

About me

I am an environmental biology researcher working across scales. My current postdoctoral work in the labs of Uri Roll and Ron Milo investigates how sea-level rise will affect global tetrapod biodiversity. I use geospatial data, coastal DEMs, and climate projections to quantify risk and inform action.

During my MSc and PhD at the Weizmann Institute of Science I engineered E. coli for synthetic CO2 fixation. I later collaborated with the International Institute for Applied Systems Analysis (IIASA) to assess how microbial protein adoption could reduce land-use emissions and support biodiversity.
